We don't decide the ad budget split in a Monday meeting anymore. The system proposes it Friday night.

The old way: someone exports last week's numbers, builds a spreadsheet, argues in a meeting, reallocates on gut.

The new way: an agent pulls spend and results from every channel — ads, CRM, analytics — every Friday. It scores each channel on cost per qualified lead (not per click — per actual SQL), flags what's decaying, and proposes next week's reallocation with the reasoning attached.

A human still approves it. But they approve a recommendation, not a blank spreadsheet.

What this changes:

→ Budget follows qualified pipeline, not last-click vanity metrics.

→ Decay gets caught in days, not at month-end when the money's already spent.

→ The growth meeting goes from building the analysis to deciding on it.

The universal lesson: AI's highest-leverage job in growth isn't writing copy. It's closing the loop between spend and outcome faster than a human can — every week, automatically.

Most teams point AI at the cheap problem (content). The expensive problem is where money gets wasted, weekly, with no one watching.

I install this kind of system with a few teams a quarter — as a project, not a hire. The full method is public: link in profile.
