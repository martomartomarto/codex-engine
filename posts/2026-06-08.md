We stopped doing win/loss analysis as a quarterly project. Now it runs every night.

Most teams review lost deals once a quarter, in a deck nobody opens twice.

We wired it into the system instead. Every recorded sales call — hundreds a month — gets transcribed, then read by an agent that tags it: objection raised, competitor mentioned, feature requested, price reaction, who stalled and why.

By Monday morning the output is a ranked list:

→ The objection that killed the most deals last week.

→ The competitor that showed up most in the losses.

→ The feature three enterprise prospects asked for, unprompted.

No one scheduled a "win/loss project." The pattern library updates itself.

What changed downstream:

→ Sales gets next week's rebuttal script from last week's real losses.

→ Product gets demand ranked by revenue at risk, not by who complained loudest.

→ Marketing gets the exact words prospects use — which becomes the copy.

The insight isn't "AI reads calls." It's that analysis stopped being an event and became a property of the system. Always on, always current, zero scheduling.

At our PropTech SaaS this replaced something that used to happen four times a year. Now it happens before we wake up.

The quarterly review is dead. Long live the nightly one.
