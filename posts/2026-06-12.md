A research agent that briefs you on any prospect before the call. Three open-source parts, one afternoon.

Everyone wants "AI that researches prospects." Here's the actual stack — no platform, no subscription:

→ Firecrawl — feed it the prospect's domain. It crawls the site, pricing, blog, careers page, and returns clean text a model can read. (The careers page alone tells you what they're building and where they're thin.)

→ browser-use — for what has no API: an agent opens LinkedIn, recent news, funding, and pulls what a static scrape can't reach.

→ A reasoning loop (the open-deep-research pattern) — it doesn't just dump data. It plans questions, reads, finds gaps, searches again, and stops when it can answer: what do they sell, to whom, what changed recently, where's the wedge.

Output: a one-page brief in the rep's inbox before the call. Company, positioning, likely pain, three tailored angles.

The whole thing is ~200 lines of glue over three free repos.

The point isn't the code. It's that "AI prospect research" isn't a product you buy — it's three commodity parts wired to your CRM. The vendors selling it as SaaS are charging you for the wiring.

Wire it once. It runs on every lead, forever.
